What billionaire investors are actually doing, straight from SEC EDGAR: 13F holdings changes across 17 famous funds, fresh activist stakes (SCHEDULE 13D), and insider open-market buys (Form 4). First edition — this one establishes the baseline.

TL;DR

  • The whales are rotating within the AI trade, not out of it — selling software mega-caps (Microsoft, Salesforce, Oracle, Snowflake) and buying the physical layer: chips, memory, fab equipment, data centers, and the power to run them.
  • Alphabet is the quiet consensus buy. Berkshire tripled its stake to $15.6B. Third Point, Lone Pine, and Bridgewater all opened or added.
  • Ackman went the other way, opening a new $2.1B Microsoft position (15% of his book) while everyone else trimmed it.
  • Druckenmiller is betting on Argentina — YPF up +433%, plus a new Argentina ETF position.
  • Insider tape this week: the entire Simon Property board bought stock together, and EV-charging maker Wallbox saw its CEO plus three directors buy $5M at once.

1. The Big Rotation: out of software, into silicon and power

The most striking pattern across Q1 13Fs (period ending March 31, filed mid-May) is how many unrelated funds made the same trade:

Sold software mega-caps:

  • Microsoft: trimmed −54% by Tiger Global, −82% by Tepper, exited entirely by Lone Pine and Third Point
  • Bridgewater exited Salesforce ($512M), Workday, and ServiceNow outright
  • Coatue exited Oracle ($865M), Snowflake ($544M), Adobe, AMD, and Arm

Bought AI hardware, memory, and fab equipment:

  • TSMC: Tiger +49% (to $1.9B), Bridgewater opened a new $364M position, Coatue holds it as their #1 at 10.8% of book
  • Memory/storage everywhere: Tepper made Micron his #2 holding (9.5%) and opened Sandisk; Druckenmiller opened Sandisk and Seagate; Bridgewater added Micron +66%; Coatue opened Micron
  • Equipment: Tiger added Applied Materials +85%; Bridgewater added KLA +39%, Teradyne +70%, Credo +220%; Lone Pine opened a $555M Teradyne position
  • Coatue opened a $1.1B Equinix (data center REIT) position and $655M of ASML

Bought the power layer:

  • Vistra: Tepper +114%, Lone Pine holds it as their #1 position
  • Lone Pine added Talen Energy +41% and opened TeraWulf, Hut 8, and Argan (power plant builder) — a straight "who builds and feeds data centers" basket
  • Coatue's #2 holding is GE Vernova (7.7% of book)

The read: the smart money isn't calling the AI trade over — they're calling the software part crowded and moving down the stack to compute, memory, and electricity. Retail is still mostly arguing about chatbot subscriptions.

2. The Alphabet accumulation

  • Berkshire added +204% to GOOGL — now a $15.6B position, 5.9% of the book, their 7th largest. They also opened a smaller new GOOG lot while exiting Visa, Mastercard, UnitedHealth, and Domino's.
  • Third Point and Lone Pine opened new GOOGL positions; Bridgewater added +26% and opened GOOG.
  • Counterpoint: Coatue trimmed −37% and Pershing Square nearly exited (−95%), so it's not unanimous — but the buyer list is heavyweight.

3. Contrarian corner

  • Ackman (Pershing Square): new $2.1B Microsoft position — 15.3% of his concentrated 11-stock book — initiated exactly while Tiger, Tepper, Lone Pine, and Third Point were cutting it. Also exited Hilton and nearly exited Alphabet.
  • Buffett bought an airline again: new $2.6B Delta position (he famously swore off airlines in 2020). Also added Lennar +43% (homebuilder value) and NYT +199%.
  • Burry watch: Scion's last 13F is still Q3 2025 — 66% of it was Palantir puts and 13.5% Nvidia puts — and he hasn't filed since (deregistered). No current data, but worth remembering the biggest AI skeptic went dark holding shorts.
  • Elliott is positioned defensively: new puts on consumer discretionary (XLY), high-yield credit (HYG), and small caps (IWM), plus new gold-miner calls (GDX) — while holding Phillips 66 and Suncor as top longs and adding HPE +47%.
  • Einhorn (now filing as DME Capital): new positions in Versant Media ($112M), Crocs, and Resideo; still 19% Green Brick Partners.
  • ValueAct: opened KKR ($303M, 5.3% of book), Spotify ($174M), and Wix.
  • Druckenmiller: besides Argentina (YPF +433%, new ARGT ETF), his #1 is still Natera (18% of book) and he added STMicro +238% while exiting his XLF financials hedge.

5. Insider conviction buys (Form 4, open-market purchases only, July 1–2 filings)

  • Wallbox (WBX): CEO + 3 directors bought $5M in the EV-charging maker (June 30)

Notable large buys: Uber added $20M to Lime (Neutron Holdings — pre-IPO scooter company, Uber now averaging up as 10% owner); RH director Carlos Alberini bought $1.8M; Hartree Partners added $4.5M of Hudson Technologies (HDSN); Bradesco's CLO bought $2M of BBD.

6. What retail isn't looking at yet

  1. Memory and storage as the AI bottleneck trade — Micron, Sandisk, Seagate showing up as new positions across four unrelated whale books is the closest thing to a coordinated signal in this data.
  2. Power infrastructure as a compounding theme, not a meme — Vistra/Talen/GE Vernova/Argan/TeraWulf across multiple books; retail talks about it, but the whales keep sizing it up.
  3. Argentina — Druckenmiller quintupling YPF and buying the country ETF is a macro bet almost nobody retail-side is positioned for.
  4. Office real estate distress as an activist hunting ground — two funds filing 13Ds on OPI the same day suggests the bottom-fishing has started.
  5. Boards buying their own beaten-down names (SPG, WBX, PSNY) while indices sit near highs — insiders see value where the narrative is coldest.
