Energizer's Biggest Outside Holder Just Swept $2M of Stock at $20

The Ramirez vehicles bought 100,000 ENR shares in five clean blocks, and Conagra's CEO put half a million of his own money into a 14-handle the same day.

ENRCAGCVPRQRTSMCHCOGFMXF

No whale alerts today. The 13D column is garbage. But the Form 4 tape (the report an officer, director, or 5%-plus owner files after trading their own stock) handed over two prints I would not skip.

The print of the day

The Ramirez family vehicles, filing as one 10% owner (Aqua Capital, Durango Capital, and a stack of Panamanian foundations), bought 100,000 shares of ENR on July 17. Five clean 20,000-share blocks between $19.94 and $20.51. Call it $2.02 million.

That is four times the size of anything else on this tape. Energizer's biggest disclosed outside holder is not trimming into weakness, he is stepping in front of it at a 20-handle. On a levered consumer staple, that is a balance-sheet vote, and it is the loudest one here.

The CEO check

John Brase, President and CEO of Conagra, bought 35,000 shares of CAG at $14.5895 on July 17. $510,632 of his own cash.

Open-market buying from a sitting CEO is the cleanest version of this signal, because nobody handed it to him. Conagra at fourteen dollars is a name the market has written off. He wants more of it.

Skip the 13D column

One new SCHEDULE 13D crossed. Research Alliance Holdings IV filed on Research Alliance Corp IV. That is a SPAC sponsor disclosing it owns its own SPAC. Plumbing. https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/2137777/0002137786-26-000012-index.html

A strategic acquirer updating a declared stake in a genomics company is a different animal than a founder filing on himself. https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1527753/0001193125-26-310289-index.html
