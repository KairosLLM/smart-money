Iconiq and a Netskope Director Drop $14M Into Their Own Stock

Two 10% owners bought Netskope hand over fist, Pat Gelsinger's crew loaded Gloo, and Mission Produce drew a $7.9M vote of confidence.

NTSKGLOOAVOCALCEVGN

No whale 13Ds, no early 13Fs, no Burry. So the real tape today is insider buying, and it is loud in two names.

NTSK (Netskope, the cybersecurity outfit that went public last year) is the print. Two 10% owners (holders of more than a tenth of the stock, the people closest to the cap table) bought on the same day. Director William Griffith took down 610,291 shares for $7.2M at $11.82. ICONIQ Strategic Partners, the Divesh Makan vehicle, matched him almost share for share, 610,091 shares for another $7.2M. That is $14.4M of open-market conviction into a post-IPO name from the two insiders who know it best. When the pre-IPO backers buy the public stock instead of selling into a lockup expiry, I pay attention.

GLOO (Gloo Holdings) is the other one, and it comes with a name. Three insiders bought $6M at $3.25. CEO Scott Beck led with $3.5M, director Derek Green added $2M, and Executive Chair Pat Gelsinger, yes, the former Intel CEO, put in $500k. A CEO and his chairman writing personal checks at a buck-and-change stock is the kind of cluster (multiple insiders buying at once, the strongest insider signal there is) I want to see.

One more single buy earns the ink. AVO (Mission Produce, the avocado grower) drew a $7.9M purchase from Globalharvest Holdings, a 10% owner, 592,957 shares at $13.28. A strategic holder adding to a beaten-down ag name. Real money, one clip.

On the 13D tape (a filing that flags a 5%-plus stake with intent to influence), Alafi Capital opened a new position in CALC (CalciMedica, a clinical-stage biotech). Alafi has backed biotech for decades, so a fresh 13D there reads as accumulation, not a trade (filing). Israel's L.I.A. Pure Capital filed a new 13D on EVGN (Evogene, a micro-cap ag-genomics name), smaller and speculative (filing).

Everything else was plumbing. Viking Global amended its BridgeBio stake with no escalation to report, and the Lionsgate share moves from Mark Rachesky were internal reshuffling that never touched the open market. Ignore both.
