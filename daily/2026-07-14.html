Four IperionX Insiders Buy Titanium the Same Day While 51Talk's CEO Drops $9.3M

A full-house cluster in a US titanium play leads the tape, a Chinese ADR founder writes the biggest check, and Commercial Metals' CEO steps in on steel.

IPXCOECMCGGALENRCYABTDICCAIABG

The tape's best print is not the biggest one. It's the cluster.

Four IPX (IperionX) insiders bought on July 10, and they did it together. Executive Chairman Todd Hannigan put in $1.88M, director Lorraine Martin added $1M, President Toby Symonds $500k, and CEO Anastasios Arima another $497k. That is $3.9M of open-market buying across the top of the house, all around $3.58 a share. When the whole C-suite and a director step in on the same day (a cluster buy, and the strongest insider signal there is), they are telling you the stock is cheap right here. IperionX is a US titanium producer aimed straight at defense and aerospace supply chains. This is the kind of critical-minerals name that shows up on insider tapes before it shows up on CNBC.

Now the big number. COE (51Talk Online Education) CEO Jack Huang bought $9.3M of his own stock on July 6, roughly 563,000 shares between $16.45 and $16.72. That dwarfs everything else on the tape in raw dollars. I hold Chinese ADRs to a higher bar because the governance is murkier and a founder can buy to prop up a floor. But $9.3M in a single session is not a gesture, it is a statement.

Two more single buys I like. CMC (Commercial Metals) President and CEO Peter Matt bought $504k on July 10 at $61.30. A steel-cycle boss buying his own shares in the open market is a clean, boring, useful signal. And GGAL (Grupo Financiero Galicia) director Silvestre Vila Moret took down $2.16M of the Argentine bank on July 7 around $5.40, a real vote that the Milei trade keeps running.

The rest in one breath. Energizer's (ENR) controlling Ramirez group added $1.23M near $20.50. A four-person C-suite cluster at disinformation-detection micro-cap CYAB (Cyabra) bought a combined $285k, small dollars but the entire management team at once. And Dreamland's (TDIC) CEO Wai Yue Seto both bought $2.4M and filed a fresh 13D (a disclosure of a stake above 5%) on July 13. Founder buying his own company, so I log it and move on. And activist Impactive Capital amended its ABG (Asbury Automotive) 13D with nothing that reads as a real escalation.
