51Talk's CEO Just Put $23 Million Into His Own Stock

Jack Huang's seven-figure buying spree leads a tape with an $8 million avocado add and a Maryland thrift where four insiders wrote checks at once.

COEAVOEWSBPRQRCADEXVII

One print on today's tape dwarfs everything else, so let's start there.

The insider tape

COE. Jack Jiajia Huang, CEO of 51Talk Online Education Group, bought roughly 1.44 million shares of his own company on June 29 at prices from $15.20 to $16.78. Total damage: $23.5 million. That is not a signaling nibble or an options exercise dressed up as conviction. That is a chief executive backing up the truck across seven separate blocks in a single session, paying up as the price moved against him. When the person with the best information in the building spends that kind of money at market, I pay attention.

AVO. Globalharvest Holdings Venture Ltd, already a 10% owner (holders that size must report their trades, which is why we see them), added 650,415 shares of Mission Produce at $12.73 on July 6. That is an $8.3 million add to an existing position in the avocado supplier, and big holders rarely reload into names they plan to trim.

EWSB. Four insiders at EWSB Bancorp bought a combined $1.7 million on June 29, led by President and CEO Charles Schmalz at $676,410. Every share went off at a flat $10.00, which tells me this is a thrift conversion offering rather than open-market buying. Still, management subscribing seven figures of their own money into their own bank is skin in the game, just discount the signal accordingly.

PRQR. ProQR Therapeutics CEO Daniel De Boer bought 150,000 shares at $1.59 on July 7, a $238,500 check in a beaten-down gene therapy name. Modest dollars, but CEOs buying sub-$2 biotech stock with their own cash is rarer than you would think.

Two prints you can ignore. Thrivent Financial dropped $10.5 million into CADEX, an interval credit fund, which is asset allocation, not conviction. And the Viking Acquisition Corp. II sponsor bought $3 million of VII units at $10.00, which is standard SPAC plumbing.

Fresh 13Ds

Two individuals, Seth Demsey and John Crystal, each filed a new 13D (a form filed when someone crosses 5% with intent to influence) on Corvex, Inc. on July 8, the same day (Demsey filing, Crystal filing). Twin same-day stakes from individuals usually means deal consideration just landed, not an activist campaign. File it under context.

Jason Sawyer also went active on GridAI Technologies Corp. (filing). An individual taking an active stake in a small grid-infrastructure name during an AI power buildout is the kind of filing that ages interestingly.
