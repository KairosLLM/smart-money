Energizer's Biggest Outside Holder Just Bought $2M in the Open

No whale 13Ds worth chasing, but a handful of size buyers kept paying up: Energizer's top holder, Tang Capital in Fulcrum, and Rick Rieder buying his own fund.

ENRVVOSFULCECATIPXBOT

No whale 13Ds worth chasing today and no insider clusters. What is left is a short list of single prints where somebody with real size kept buying in the open. Those are the ones I actually read.

Start with ENR (Energizer Holdings). The 10% owner group tied to Alfredo Diez Ramirez (Aqua Capital and a stack of related vehicles, the single largest outside holder) bought 100,000 shares across five prints on July 13, all around $20.18, for just over $2 million. This is a Form 4, the filing an insider or 5%-plus owner files after trading, and it is a real open-market add at market price. When your biggest outside shareholder keeps paying up like this, that is conviction you can price.

Next, a fresh SCHEDULE 13D (a stake over 5% filed when the holder plans to get active) landed on VVOS (Vivos Therapeutics) from V-Co Investors 4 LLC on July 15. Pair it with the tape. 10% owner Michael Skaff disclosed buying 5.5 million shares of the same name at $0.456, roughly $3.2 million total. Two separate parties accumulating a sub-dollar sleep-apnea name in the same week is the kind of confluence I stop on, even if the price tag screams lottery ticket. Filing: SEC.

FULC (Fulcrum Therapeutics) is the one I like best. Tang Capital Management, Kevin Tang's shop and a serial biotech accumulator that tends to show up ahead of hard catalysts, added roughly $891k across four July 13 prints near $3.56. Tang does not dabble in a name he is not building.

Two more. Rick Rieder, BlackRock's fixed-income CIO, personally put $567k into ECAT (the firm's own ESG closed-end fund) at $15.86 on July 13. And former Corning CFO Tony Tripeny wrote a $1 million check for IPX (IperionX), the US titanium-metal play, as a director on July 14. A manager buying his own fund and a director going seven figures both rhyme with "this looks cheap to me."

What I am ignoring. The $10 million "purchase" by RoboStrategy president Andrew Kang in BOT is a suspiciously round number in a barely-public shell that reads like founder plumbing, not a signal. And Warren Buffett's Berkshire 13D/A is his usual annual share reshuffle for charity, no new money.
