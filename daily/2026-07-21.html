No whale alerts on this tape. Two days of filings, one strategic 13D worth your time, and one CEO writing a very large personal check.

A software company just filed a 13D on SPAR Group

ReposiTrak filed a fresh SCHEDULE 13D on SPAR Group on July 20. A 13D is the form you file when you cross 5% of a company and intend to actually do something about it, as opposed to the passive 13G that index funds file by the truckload.

What makes this one interesting is that the filer is not a fund at all. It is an operating company, a supply chain compliance software outfit, taking a declared stake in a retail merchandising services business it plausibly wants to own. Strategics do not cross 5% for the dividend.

Filing: https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/50471/0001437749-26-023888-index.html

The other two new 13Ds are founders filing on their own paper, Konstantakopoulos at Costamare Bulkers and Thien Chiet Chai at Founder Group. Internal plumbing. Ignore both.

The insider tape

Jack Jiajia Huang, CEO of 51Talk Online Education, bought 495,360 shares of COE across three fills on July 9 at $16.71 to $17.24. Call it $8.48 million of his own money. That is the print of the day and it is not close. A Form 4 (the disclosure an officer or director files after trading their own stock) showing a CEO buying eight figures on the open market is the highest-quality signal this tape produces, and Huang paid up into the offer rather than lowballing bids.

Fairmount Funds Management took 853,450 shares of CBIO at $14.50 on July 16, about $12.4 million, plus a second tranche of 525,897 shares struck at a tenth of a cent. Fairmount sits as both director and 10% owner at Crescent Biopharma, which puts them on the same reporting hook as management. Their 13D/A the same day is just the paperwork catching up. Dedicated healthcare money adding size at $14.50 is a real vote.

Michael Heinrich, executive chairman of ZeroStack, picked up 4,608,575 shares of ZSTK at $0.7549 on July 20, roughly $3.48 million. Seven-figure check from the chairman, sub-dollar tape. The size is real. So is what the price is telling you.

RA Capital added 160,354 shares of ARTV around $8.93 on July 16, about $1.43 million. Kevin Tang and Tang Capital bought 167,616 shares of FULC at $3.52 to $3.56, roughly $595,000. Tang buys busted biotech for the cash sitting on the balance sheet, so when he accumulates, somebody has already run the liquidation math.

Outside biotech there was one print worth naming. Director Homer Livingston III bought 26,343 shares of FCEL at $18.79, about $495,000.

Now the one to throw out. Lincoln Financial put $25 million into LNBIX, its own Bain Capital credit fund. Biggest dollar figure on the tape and the least informative thing on it. That is seed capital, not conviction.

No cluster buys today, meaning no company had two or more insiders buying inside the window. One CEO going big is what you get.
