ICONIQ Buys $3.8M More Netskope in the Open Market Under $12.50

A pre-IPO backer of the cybersecurity name added to its own position at the open, the one print on today's tape that actually says something.

NTSKGLBSNMMARTV

Skip the 13D pile first. A 13D is the filing you make when you cross 5% with intent to do something. Today's two fresh ones are both plumbing. Brown Brothers Harriman and AMG New York Holdings both filed on the same AMG BBH Asset-Backed Credit Fund, which is affiliate housekeeping between related shops, not an activist landing. Garden Investment Management filed a real one on Midera Food Processing, an obscure micro-cap I have no track record to trust (filing). Note it, do not chase it.

Now the tape that matters.

On July 10, ICONIQ Strategic Partners bought 306,399 shares of NTSK (Netskope) across two lots at $12.26 and $12.42, about $3.77M. Read the raw feed and it looks like a two-insider cluster worth $7.5M. It is not. The exact same shares show up once under the ICONIQ fund entities and once under partner William Griffith, both filed as 10% owners on a Form 4 (the report an insider or big holder files after trading). One buy, double-counted. Call it $3.8M.

Here is why I care anyway. ICONIQ backed Netskope before it went public last year, and here they are adding in the open market at roughly $12, not taking a courtesy allocation. A crossover sponsor paying up for more of a name it already knows cold is the closest thing to conviction this tape offers.

Next, shipping. Georgios Feidakis, chairman of GLBS (Globus Maritime), picked up 221,166 shares for about $603k at $2.70 to $2.75 (dated June 18, filed now). A controlling shareholder putting $600k into his own $2 dry-bulk micro-cap clears our single-buy bar cleanly. Late, but real.

Then NMM (Navios Maritime Partners), where CEO and chairwoman Angeliki Frangou added 3,334 shares for about $252k around $75 on July 10. Frangou buys her own stock like a reflex, so discount it a notch, but a C-suite open-market add (that is the top brass buying, not options vesting) still counts.

One more for the biotech watchers. RA Capital, Peter Kolchinsky's shop, added about $194k of ARTV (Artiva Biotherapeutics) near $8.95 as a 10% owner. Small, and below our headline bar, but RA rarely nibbles without a thesis behind it.

Everything else is noise. Horizon Kinetics kept buying its one-share and few-hundred-dollar tokens of Texas Pacific Land and RENN Fund, Stilwell tossed $4,300 at Peoples Financial, and a handful of sub-$30k director buys dotted the small caps. Ignore all of it.
