The Biggest Buy on the Tape Is Avocados: $15.8M Into Mission Produce

Globalharvest piles into Mission Produce, Lynrock Lake goes active on Yext, Renesas discloses a stake in SiTime, and Energy Fuels' CEO writes a near-million-dollar check.

AVOYEXTSITMUUUUGGALENRWRAPNMM

The print of the day is produce. Globalharvest Holdings Venture Ltd, already a 10% owner of Mission Produce (AVO), bought 1,179,087 more shares on July 7 at $13.40 to $13.42. That is a $15.8 million check in a single session. The same day it amended its Schedule 13D (the form you file when you own over 5% and reserve the right to lean on management), so this is a control-sized holder still adding, on the record. Filing

Fresh 13Ds

Yext (YEXT). Lynrock Lake LP filed a Schedule 13D on July 9. A 13D is the active form. Funds that just want to own the stock file the passive version, so Lynrock is telling you it reserves the right to get loud at the search-and-listings software company. Filing

SiTime (SITM). Renesas Electronics America, the US arm of the Japanese chip giant, filed a 13D on the precision-timing chipmaker. Strategic corporates do not file active stakes in adjacent chip companies for fun. The menu here is short: deeper partnership or an eventual takeout. Filing

Skip the rest. Securitize drew two 13Ds from its own people, including CEO Carlos Domingo, which is post-listing paperwork. Peak XV registering its seed stake in Horizon Quantum is the same story. Ignore all of it.

The insider tape

Energy Fuels (UUUU) is the cluster of the day (two or more insiders buying at once, which is when Form 4s, the insider trade reports, actually mean something). President and CEO Ross Bhappu bought 74,000 shares at $13.08 on July 7, a $967,920 personal check. Director Bruce Hansen added $50,780 the next day. A CEO putting nearly a million of his own money into a uranium producer has my full attention.

Grupo Financiero Galicia (GGAL). Director Silvestre Vila Moret bought roughly 403,000 shares of the Argentine bank on June 26 at $5.11 to $5.27, $2.09 million total. It just hit the tape this week, and a director buying eight figures of pesos worth of his own bank in dollars is conviction.

Energizer (ENR). The holding entities tied to Alfredo Jose Diez Ramirez, a 10% owner group, added 80,000 shares at $20.25 to $20.81, $1.64 million. A major holder quietly feeding on a beaten-down consumer staple.

Two quick ones. WRAP got a small cluster, director John Shulman at $110,000 and Executive Chairman and CEO Scot Cohen at $23,914, and it only makes the cut because they bought together. NMM CEO Angeliki Frangou added another $249,016 of Navios Maritime Partners at $73.74 to $76.14. She buys her own units constantly, and the consistency is the point.

The AMG and Brown Brothers Harriman purchases in their own credit fund are seed-capital plumbing. Pass.
