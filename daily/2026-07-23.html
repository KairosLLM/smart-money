RA Capital piles $6.9M into Parabilis while a whole Jersey boardroom buys at $10

Peter Kolchinsky's fund keeps adding to a small-cap biotech, sixteen Columbia Financial insiders subscribe in lockstep, and a $93M CEO "buy" turns out to mean less than the number suggests.

PBLSCLBKHDSNNMMTOI

No whale 13Ds, no early 13Fs, no Burry. Straight from SEC EDGAR, the real signal today lives in the insider tape.

Start with RA Capital. Peter Kolchinsky and Rajeev Shah's healthcare fund (a marquee biotech crossover shop, the kind that follows a drug program from private rounds into the public float) bought $6.9M of PBLS (Parabilis Medicines) on July 20. Six tranches, 244,593 shares, prices climbing from $27.13 to $30.00. They already sit on the board as a 10% owner, and they paid up into strength rather than waiting for a dip. When the people with the deepest read on a small-cap pipeline add this aggressively, I listen.

Now the cluster. CLBK (Columbia Financial) logged sixteen insiders buying $4.77M, every single share at exactly $10.00. That price flatness means a subscription in the New Jersey thrift's stock offering, not open-market conviction. Even so, this is the whole house writing personal checks. CEO Thomas Kemly put in $530k, CFO Thomas Splaine $500k, COO Steven Klein $500k, and the full board lined up behind them. A conversion buy is softer than chasing the tape, but sixteen executives funding their own recapitalization still tells you where they think book value goes from here.

The biggest number today is a mirage. Sterling Real Estate Trust CEO Kenneth Regan "bought" $92.9M (3,644,624 units at $25.50) on July 21. Sterling is a non-traded REIT, so that $25.50 is a fixed NAV subscription price, not a market bid anyone else can hit. Impressive line, zero tradable signal. Move on.

The smaller prints worth clocking: Hartree Partners added $392k to HDSN (Hudson Technologies) as a 10% owner, staying long the refrigerant-pricing story. Navios chief Angeliki Frangou bought another $253k of NMM (Navios Maritime Partners), a name she scoops up on nearly every pullback. Jorey Chernett took $95k more of TOI (Oncology Institute) as a 10% owner.

On the activist side, Saba Capital amended its 13D (the filing a 5%-plus owner uses to flag intent) on BlackRock ESG Capital Allocation Trust, and Legion Partners amended on NN Inc. Both are known agitators. Read these as housekeeping unless a later filing shows they raised their stakes, which today's does not.

The trade of the day is one name. RA Capital wants more Parabilis, and they know it better than you do.
