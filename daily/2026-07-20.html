Sumitomo Drops Another $319M on Jefferies

Japan's largest bank keeps buying its Wall Street partner, Prudential loads $60M of energy infrastructure, and Elevance's CEO steps in as managed care bleeds.

JEFTACTTYGELVSTDNPDILILA

One print towers over everything else on the tape.

Sumitomo Mitsui Financial Group bought 5,906,542 shares of JEF at $53.96 on July 15, a $318.7M open-market buy disclosed on a Form 4 (the filing any board member or 10%-plus owner must submit within two days of trading). Japan's biggest bank sits on Jefferies' board and has spent years building this alliance from a handshake into a real ownership stake. A third of a billion dollars in a single day is not a rebalance. It is a strategic partner leaning harder into the relationship at a price it clearly likes.

That is the read of the day. Everything below is smaller.

The one 13D worth your time

Poplar Point Capital Management filed a fresh SCHEDULE 13D on TransAct Technologies (TACT), the filing an investor must submit within 10 days of crossing 5% with intent to influence the company. Poplar Point is a concentrated value shop, and TransAct is a beaten-down maker of casino and food-service tech. A patient fund putting its name on the line at a micro-cap this quiet is the kind of setup that pays off in quarters, not days. Filing: https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1588273/0001398344-26-012268-index.html

Ignore the Columbus Circle SPAC sponsor filing. That is plumbing.

The insider tape

Prudential Financial bought $60M of Tortoise Energy Infrastructure (TYG) at a flat $10, two 3-million-share tranches on July 15. As a 10% owner accumulating a closed-end energy pipeline fund, Prudential is quietly telling you where it wants yield and hard-asset exposure right now.

Managed care has been a graveyard, which makes the Elevance Health (ELV) cluster interesting. CEO Gail Boudreaux put up $1.0M across two buys at $366 to $368, and director Ramiro Peru added $366k. When the sitting CEO buys seven figures of her own hammered stock in the open market, that is conviction you can measure.

Decisive Point Group bought $19.1M of Standard Nuclear (STDN) at $15. Another 10%-owner bet on the nuclear buildout that keeps drawing serious money.

Two quick hits. PIMCO's Christian Stracke bought $1.64M of the PIMCO Dynamic Income Fund (PDI), a credit heavyweight backing his own book. And Liberty Latin America chairman Michael Fries added $1.0M of LILA at $20.39.

What to skip: Manulife shuffled $79M between four of its own affiliates into the John Hancock GA Mortgage Trust. That is insurance plumbing, not a signal. Same for German American Bancorp's directors matching $20k apiece.
