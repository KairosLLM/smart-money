Two Dollar-Stock Directors Just Wrote Million-Dollar Checks

Cardiff Oncology and Vivani insiders each put $1M into sub-$1.50 biotech, while Thrivent quietly dropped $4M into an Ares credit fund.

CRDFVANIARDCBOLDBAFNCMMB

No whale 13Ds, no early 13Fs, no Scion. So the story is the insider tape, and it is louder than the price tags suggest.

CRDF (Cardiff Oncology) is the real print. Director Gary Pace bought 687,285 shares for a clean $1,000,000 at $1.455 on July 14, and CEO Mani Mohindru added $50,000 the same day. Two insiders, one date, roughly $1.05M into a microcap oncology name trading under $1.50. A cluster (multiple insiders buying the same window) at a director's own million-dollar size is about as direct a signal as this tape produces. When the board writes personal checks this big into a broken chart, they either know something or they are very stubborn.

VANI (Vivani Medical) is the echo. Director and 10% owner Gregg Williams took down 793,650 shares for $999,999 at $1.26 on July 15. Same playbook, different name. A near-million from someone who already owns a tenth of the company is not a token gesture.

Now the big dollar figure, with an asterisk. ARDC (Ares Dynamic Credit Allocation Fund) saw Thrivent Financial for Lutherans, a 10% owner, buy 160,000 shares for $4,000,000 flat at $25 on July 14. Real money, but it is an institution accumulating a closed-end credit fund at NAV, not a founder betting on his own turnaround. Read it as steady credit demand, not a conviction spike.

Same flavor over at Diameter Dynamic Credit Fund, where Co-President Scott Goodwin put $1,250,000 into his own vehicle at $10.06. Insider seeding the fund. Nice to see, not a stock call.

One name to file away: Tang Capital, Kevin Tang's biotech shop, kept nibbling BOLD (Boundless Bio) as a 10% owner, roughly $155,000 across three prints at $2.43 to $2.44 on July 14. Small on its own, but Tang tends to keep buying.

On the 13D side it was closed-end-fund activism and community banks, not household names. Karpus Management filed on an AllianceBernstein muni income fund, and bank specialist Kenneth Lehman filed on BAFN (BayFirst Financial) here. Biotech fund HBM Healthcare filed on CMMB (Chemomab) here. Worth a bookmark, not a chase.

Skip the Sterling Real Estate Trust unit buys (flat $25.50 plan purchases) and the sub-$4k plumbing.
