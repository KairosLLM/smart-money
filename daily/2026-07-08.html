Allseas Takes Another 7.3 Million Shares of TMC

The deep-sea miner's biggest strategic backer keeps stacking stock, a Marchex co-founder goes active, and two First Trust officers put $8.4 million into their own funds.

TMCMCHXVFLEXFTECXPSBDGFRIG

The print that matters today is TMC. Allseas Group, the Swiss offshore contractor that is both The Metals Company's engineering partner and its largest strategic holder, reported acquiring another 7,305,567 shares on July 1 and amended its SCHEDULE 13D (the form a holder files when it owns over 5% with intent to influence the company) the same day. The Form 4 lists no price, which usually means a contractual or warrant transaction rather than open-market buying. Either way, the partner that actually builds the mining ships keeps converting its relationship into equity, and that is the kind of alignment I want to see in a story stock. Filing here.

One more fresh 13D deserves a line. Russell C. Horowitz, who co-founded MCHX (Marchex) and ran it for years, filed a brand-new 13D on July 7 rather than another passive amendment. Founder filings are usually paperwork, but a founder switching to an active schedule on a forgotten micro-cap is how take-private stories start. Filing here.

The rest of the 13D stack is skippable. John Calamos filed on his own firm's new hedged-strategies fund and the Osprey III sponsor filed on its own SPAC. That is plumbing, not positioning.

The insider tape

The biggest dollars are a cluster (multiple insiders buying the same name, historically the strongest insider signal) at First Trust. President Michael D. Peck and Treasurer Chad Eisenberg put a combined $6.5 million into VFLEX at $27.66 and another $1.9 million into FTECX at $9.42, buys dated back in April and only now hitting EDGAR. I discount fund officers seeding their own vehicles. It is real skin in the game, but it is a business commitment, not a market call.

The cleaner signals are smaller:

  • PSBD: Martin C. Bicknell, the Mariner Wealth CEO who owns over 10% of Palmer Square Capital BDC, bought another 61,484 shares on July 6 at $10.47 to $10.56, about $647,000. A 10% owner adding in the open market at these levels is a straight bet on the credit book.
  • GF: Saba Capital took another $507,000 of New Germany Fund at $11.60 on July 2. Boaz Weinstein grinding a closed-end fund discount is a playbook, not a thesis, but he only grinds when the gap pays.
  • RIG: Transocean director Chad C. Deaton bought 35,000 shares at $4.95 on July 2, $173,250. A six-figure open-market buy from a board member in an out-of-favor offshore driller is exactly the kind of quiet print this tape exists to catch.
