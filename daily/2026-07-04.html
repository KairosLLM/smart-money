Wallbox's board and CEO just bought $5M together, and Uber quietly put $20M into Lime

A board-plus-CEO cluster at multi-year lows is the only print that matters on a sleepy holiday tape. Two biotech 13Ds and a $6M director buy round it out.

Quiet holiday wire. No whale 13Ds, no early 13Fs, no Burry sightings. But one insider print today is the kind I actually care about, so let's get to it.

The only real story: WBX

WBX (Wallbox N.V.). Four insiders bought ~$5.0M of the EV-charging maker on the same day. Three directors at $1.36M each, plus CEO Enric Asuncion Escorsa adding $887k of his own. That is a cluster buy (multiple insiders buying together, the strongest insider signal there is), it is board-plus-CEO in unison, and it lands with the stock near multi-year lows. Insiders don't coordinate seven figures each into a falling chart for fun. Highest-conviction print on the tape, not close.

Fresh 13Ds

A 13D is an activist-scale stake, 5% or more with intent to do something about it. Two crossed today, both concentrated biotech money bottom-fishing:

  • CALC (CalciMedica). Bering Partners II GP filed a new SCHEDULE 13D on the immunology small-cap. First-time activist-scale money in a name this size gets my attention. Filing
  • PASG (Passage BIO). Lynx1 Capital Management opened a 13D on the gene-therapy name that has spent the year trading near cash value. Someone just decided the bottom of the range is investable. Filing

The rest of today's 13D wire was plumbing and I'm not making you read it: Gould Investors' twin filings on BRT Apartments and OLP (internal, affiliated), a Jefferies-to-Jefferies BDC shuffle, and a Hugreat/Alpex SPAC stake. Skip.

One amendment I do care about: LFCR (Lifecore Biomedical) drew same-day 13D/A activity from both 22NW and Legion Partners. No fresh escalation disclosed, but two activists touching the same small-cap on the same day means the seat-holders are still working.

The rest of the insider tape

  • CREX (Creative Realities). CEO Richard Mills bought $700k and CFO Tamra Koshewa $100k of the digital-signage micro-cap at $3.50. An $800k C-suite cluster in a company this small is real money to these people.
  • LIME (Neutron Holdings). Uber put $20M to work, 800k shares at $25, lifting its stake in the micromobility operator. A strategic corporate buyer averaging UP is a different animal than an insider buy, and the size demands notice.
  • BNR (Burning Rock Biotech). Director Yusheng Han disclosed $6.2M of open-market buying across the June 11 session in the China cancer-diagnostics name.
  • HDSN (Hudson Technologies). Hartree Partners, already a 10% owner, added $4.5M of the refrigerant reclaimer at ~$5.85.
  • BBD (Bank Bradesco). CLO Julio Cesar Bueno bought $2.0M of the Brazilian bank.
  • PSBD (Palmer Square Capital BDC). 10% owner Martin Bicknell added $845k near $10.55.
  • PSNY (Polestar). Three directors added a combined ~$78k. Small, but board buying is board buying.
