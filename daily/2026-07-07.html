Charles Gillman Plants an Activist Flag in TransAct While YPF's Top Brass Buys Its Own Stock

A veteran small-cap activist files a fresh 13D on the casino-printer maker, YPF's chairman and upstream chief put half a million on the tape, and a Brazilian power director drops $4.3 million.

One real activist print, one C-suite cluster worth your attention, and a lot of plumbing to ignore. Here is the tape.

The 13D that matters

Charles Gillman filed a fresh SCHEDULE 13D on TransAct Technologies (TACT) on July 6 (filing). A 13D is the loud form, filed when you cross 5% of a company and intend to do something about it, and Gillman does not file quiet paperwork. He is a career small-cap activist who has fought his way onto dozens of microcap boards. TransAct makes transaction printers and terminals for casinos and restaurants, a sleepy niche with real customers, exactly the kind of underloved balance sheet activists like to shake.

The rest of the 13D pile was amendments from founders, family holders, and fund vehicles rebalancing their own paperwork. Nothing there changes a thesis.

Insiders: energy executives are writing checks

YPF (YPF) gave us the best cluster of the day (two or more insiders buying the same stock in the same window, which is rarer and louder than one). Chairman and CEO Horacio Daniel Marin bought 2,840 shares at $46.35 for $131,634 on July 2, and his VP of Upstream, Matias Osvaldo Farina, bought 8,875 shares across two prints for roughly $401,000 the same day. The two men running Argentina's flagship oil company put over half a million dollars of their own money into the stock in one session. I take that at face value.

Same sector, different hemisphere: Pedro Batista de Lima Filho, a director at AXIA Energia (AXIA3), Brazil's power giant, bought about 415,000 shares around $10.25 on July 1, roughly $4.3 million in total. That is the largest genuine open-market buy on the tape and it barely made a headline anywhere.

Angeliki Frangou did her usual thing at Navios Maritime Partners (NMM), buying 3,511 units across three prints between $71.59 and $74.48 for about $256,000. She buys her own units constantly, so one purchase proves little, but the pattern of a CEO steadily accumulating for years is its own signal.

A footnote on the other cluster: three officers of BlackRock Monticello Debt REIT bought $598,000 combined on July 1, every share at exactly $25.3754. Identical pricing means a subscription at NAV, not three people hitting the ask. File it away, do not trade it.

Ignore this one

SaverOne (SVRE) shows a $1.1 billion "insider buy" from VisionWave Holdings. That is merger mechanics, 326 million shares changing hands in a deal, not conviction. Skip it.
