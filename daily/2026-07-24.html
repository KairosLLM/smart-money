51Talk's CEO Just Dropped $10.5M Into His Own Stock

Jack Huang writes a ten-million-dollar check on COE while Glenbrook opens an activist SenesTech stake and Energizer's biggest holder keeps buying.

COESNESENRARTVRBKBBYRN

The only headline print on the tape today is a big one. Jack Jiajia Huang, CEO of COE (51Talk Online Education Group), bought $10.5 million of his own stock on July 14. Five tranches, 622,140 shares, prices from $16.56 to $17.23. That is a Form 4 (the filing an insider must submit within two days of trading their own company's shares).

Nobody accidentally buys ten million dollars. When the boss of a beaten-down Chinese education name writes a check that size, I want to know what he sees that the tape doesn't.

Now the fresh 13D. SNES (SenesTech): Glenbrook Capital Management filed a Schedule 13D (a stake over 5% carrying activist intent, versus the passive 13G) on the rodent-birth-control microcap. A real fund taking a disclosed activist position in a company this small usually wants something to change. Filing.

Skip the other two 13Ds. Michael Field on HST Global and an individual on Metals Royalty are microcap plumbing, not smart money.

ENR (Energizer Holdings): the Ramirez group (Aqua Capital and a cluster of affiliated foundations), already a 10% owner (they hold more than a tenth of the company), added $2.43 million across seven buys at $19.67 to $20.96 on July 21. Quiet accumulation by the biggest holder of a name sitting near multi-year lows is exactly what this letter hunts for.

ARTV (Artiva Biotherapeutics): RA Capital Management bought $2.58 million and amended its 13D the same day. RA is one of the sharpest biotech crossover shops around, and when it adds to a clinical-stage name near $9.50 while refreshing its activist filing, that is conviction, not a rebalance. Filing.

Then the clusters (multiple insiders buying inside the same window, the cleanest signal on the whole tape). RBKB (Rhinebeck Bancorp): four insiders, CEO Matthew Smith, directors Nancy Patzwahl and Suzanne Loughlin, and commercial-banking head Michael Vitale, bought $650k combined at roughly $12 on July 22. Four checks into a sleepy Hudson Valley thrift on the same day is loud. BYRN (Byrna Technologies): CEO Conn Davis plus directors TJ Kennedy and Herbert Hughes put in $253k around $3.45 on the less-lethal weapons maker.

Ignore the noise. A SPAC sponsor bought $6.9 million of its own $10.00 units and a UAW retiree trust dollar-cost-averaged $9.9 million into a non-traded BDC. Both are mechanics running on autopilot, not anyone making a bet.
