One Buyer Took Down $32 Million of Mission Produce This Week

Globalharvest paid up for AVO three sessions running while shipping insiders and Pat Gelsinger opened their own checkbooks.

Welcome to the first Pattern Watch, the Saturday read where I line up the whole week of filings and look for what single days can't show. There were 381 open-market insider buys on EDGAR this week. Three patterns matter, and one of them involves avocados.

The avocado grab

AVO first, because it is not close. Globalharvest Holdings Venture Ltd is a 10% owner of Mission Produce, which means every purchase hits a Form 4 (the insider trade report, filed within two business days). This week they filed three.

Monday: 650,415 shares at $12.73. Tuesday: 1,179,087 shares at $13.40 and $13.42. Thursday: 592,957 more at $13.28. That is 2.42 million shares and $31.97 million in five sessions, and the buyer paid up every time.

They also amended their Schedule 13D on Thursday (the form you file when you hold over 5% of a company with intent, and amendments flag stake changes). The Daily Tape caught Thursday's slug in isolation. The full week shows a holder already above 10% absorbing every share the market offered at rising prices. Nobody does that casually.

Shipping insiders are all doing something

No single day looked like a sector signal. The week does. Angeliki Frangou, CEO and Chairwoman of Navios Maritime Partners (NMM), bought her own units on July 1 and again on July 7, $505,400 total, paying from $71.59 up to $76.14. She bought strength, not weakness.

Around her: Diana Shipping amended its 13D on Genco Shipping (GNK) twice on July 8 alone. Towerview LLC amended on StealthGas (GASS) Friday, and Astaris Capital amended on KNOT Offshore Partners (KNOP) Monday. Four shipping names drew insider or 5% paper in five sessions, and shipping insiders have a long record of timing their own cycle better than anyone outside it.

Pat Gelsinger bought what?

Friday's filings delivered the week's best cluster buy (multiple insiders buying the same stock in the same window, the strongest Form 4 pattern there is). Gloo Holdings (GLOO) took $6.0 million from three of its own people. CEO Scott Beck bought $3.5 million at $3.25, director Derek Green added $2.0 million, and Pat Gelsinger, the former Intel CEO who is now Gloo's Executive Chair and Head of Technology, put in $500,000.

It was filed Friday, after the morning editions. You are reading it here first.

Funds doing double duty

Lynrock Lake had a week. A fresh 13D on Yext (YEXT) Thursday and an amended 13D on Teradata (TDC) Wednesday. One fund planting flags in two unloved enterprise software names inside 48 hours reads as a thesis: they think cheap software is done going down.

Netskope (NTSK) printed a strange twin. Two 10% owners, William J.G. Griffith and the ICONIQ Strategic Partners vehicles, each bought roughly $7.2 million on July 8 at an identical $11.824 per share. The matched price and size says negotiated block, and the stock sits well under its IPO price. Still, $14.4 million of existing-holder money catching that knife is a vote.

The repeat buyers

Martin Bicknell kept adding to Palmer Square Capital BDC (PSBD), $1.49 million across July 1 and July 6. Eliyahou Harari bought CapsoVision (CV) at $7.35 on June 26, then came back July 8 for nearly six times the shares at $6.09. That is averaging down with intent.

Kevin Tang's Tang Capital added to Boundless Bio (BOLD) twice for $311,560 total, and Tang circling a broken biotech has ended in buyouts before. ATLAS Infrastructure added $302,226 of H2O America (HTO) across two days. And Energy Fuels (UUUU) earns a line: President and CEO Ross Bhappu bought $967,920 of stock at $13.08 on July 7 with a director alongside him. A uranium CEO writing a near-million-dollar personal check is conviction you can measure.

What you can ignore

Securitize's two July 9 13Ds came from CEO Carlos Domingo and William Bradford Stephens, founders disclosing stakes in a newly listed company. That is paperwork on positions they already had. Same story at Perfect Corp (PERF), where founder Alice Chang and parent CyberLink amended in lockstep, and at Beasley Broadcast, where the filers are literally named Beasley. Renesas did file a fresh 13D on SiTime (SITM), a strategic corporate stake I will come back to if it grows.

The week in one paragraph

The strongest money this week chased real assets: $32 million of avocados at rising prices, a shipping complex where the Navios CEO and Diana Shipping's board moved in the same five sessions, and a uranium chief buying seven figures of his own stock. The growthier prints came from holders defending their own deals, ICONIQ at Netskope and the Gloo insiders at $3.25. Lynrock Lake's double filing says at least one sharp fund now treats old software as a value trade. Watch AVO above everything else, because nobody spends $32 million in a week by accident, and next Saturday I grade all of it.
